Who this is for

This handbook is written for engineers who need to actually build and deploy ML systems — not for researchers benchmarking on toy datasets. If you're working on turbomachinery, combustion, chemical processes, structural health monitoring, energy systems, or any physical asset where wrong predictions have real consequences, this is for you.

⚙️

Physics-first, not data-first

Every method is framed around what you know from physics and what you're trying to learn from data.

🔬

Equations that mean something

No hand-waving. Every key formula is derived, explained, and connected to an engineering interpretation.

💻

Working code, not pseudocode

Python implementations using PyTorch, GPflow, BoTorch, scikit-learn — libraries you'd actually use.

🚀

Production-oriented

ONNX export, INT8 quantisation, MLOps, certification evidence packages — the stuff that matters at deployment.

📐

Decision tables, not vague guidance

When to use GP vs XGBoost, KF vs EnKF, EI vs TuRBO — clear criteria, not "it depends".

🔒

Safety-critical aware

DO-178C, ISO 26262, ASME V&V — how certification requirements shape every design decision.
