About me

I'm a Senior Engineering Data Scientist at Baker Hughes, working at the intersection of physics-based modelling and machine learning for energy systems. Seven-plus years of building things that actually run in production — from virtual sensors on turbomachinery test rigs to 3D surrogate models that cut FEM/CFD runtimes by 50–90%.

My sweet spot is hybrid modelling: taking the physics you already trust (thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, PDEs) and making it fast, uncertainty-aware, and continuously updating with real sensor data. I hold a Ph.D. from UNIBO in Automotive for Intelligent Mobility, where I worked on AI-augmented CFD for internal combustion engines in cooperation with Maserati.

I also wrote a technical handbook on applied ML for engineering — all 23 chapters are available as readable posts in the Blog tab above.
