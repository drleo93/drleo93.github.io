About me

I'm a Senior Engineering Data Scientist at Baker Hughes, working at the intersection of physics-based modelling and machine learning for energy systems. Seven-plus years building things that actually run in production — from hybrid virtual sensors on turbomachinery test rigs to 3D surrogate models cutting FEM/CFD runtimes by 50–90%.

My sweet spot is grey-box modelling: taking the physics you already trust (thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, PDEs) and making it fast, uncertainty-aware, and continuously updating with real sensor data. I hold a Ph.D. from UNIBO in Automotive for Intelligent Mobility, working on AI-augmented CFD for internal combustion engines in cooperation with Maserati.

I also wrote a technical handbook on applied ML for engineering. 23 chapters, every one self-contained with equations, working code, and decision tables — written for engineers who have to actually deploy this stuff. Read the chapters →

What I'm working on

  • Scientific ML & Surrogate Modelling

    GP, PINN, FNO, DeepONet — fast physics-faithful surrogates that replace FEM/CFD queries in optimisation and digital-twin loops.

  • Hybrid Virtual Sensors & Anomaly Detection

    Grey-box architectures (physics model + ML residual) for real-time estimation. CUSUM, Isolation Forest, LSTM autoencoders for turbomachinery monitoring.

  • Bayesian Optimisation & Engineering Design

    Multi-objective BO (qEHVI), Bayesian RBDO, surrogate-assisted MDO. Reduced calibration times by 50–90% across multiple product lines.

  • 3D AI & Neural Operators

    POD-ROM, FNO, GNN for full-field CFD/FEM predictions. Making spatial ML tractable for real engineering geometries at production latencies.
